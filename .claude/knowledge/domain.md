# Domain Knowledge

> **Purpose:** Accumulated domain knowledge, research findings, and experimentation results that inform design and implementation decisions. This file is the institutional memory for non-obvious facts about the problem space — things that are not derivable from the code itself.
>
> **Producers:** The `researcher` role writes entries after a structured research phase. The `analyst` role may add domain facts surfaced during discovery.
>
> **Consumers:** `architect` (before making design decisions), `analyst` (during discovery), `/onboard` (to avoid re-discovering known facts in subsequent sessions).
>
> **Column definitions:**
> - `Date` — ISO date the finding was recorded (YYYY-MM-DD)
> - `Topic` — the subject area or question the finding addresses
> - `Finding` — the fact, conclusion, or insight; stated precisely
> - `Confidence` — how well-supported the finding is:
>   - `ESTABLISHED` — consensus or peer-reviewed; treat as fact
>   - `EVIDENCE` — strong supporting evidence but not fully settled; prefer over alternatives without explicit justification
>   - `HYPOTHESIS` — plausible and worth assuming, but not yet tested in this project; flag when acting on it
>   - `SPECULATION` — weak basis; do not design on this without validation
> - `Source` — citation, URL, or `internal knowledge` if based on Claude's training data with no external reference
